Logic Gates


The logic gates are the main structural part of a digital system. Logic Gates are a block of hardware that produces signals of binary 1 or 0 when input logic requirements are satisfied. Each gate has a distinct graphic symbol, and its operation can be described by means of algebraic expressions. The seven basic logic gates includes: AND, OR, XOR, NOT, NAND, NOR, and XNOR. The relationship between the input-output binary variables for each gate can be represented in tabular form by a truth table. Each gate has one or two binary input variables designated by A and B and one binary output variable designated by x.
